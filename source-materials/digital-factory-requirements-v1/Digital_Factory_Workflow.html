Reading the diagram

  • Solid arrows are the normal forward flow. Dotted arrows are recovery paths back into the flow (re-allocation, re-presenting a correct part, loading a valid standard).
  • Hard-stops (red) cannot be clicked past by an operator at the bench. The two cloud hard-blocks (stages 7 and 12) have no override at all — a documented management decision coupling throughput to cloud availability.
  • Supervisor / manager actions (purple) are the only override points: part re-allocation, calibration disposition, and scrap (manager tier only).
  • Calibration shows the full loop: standard-validity hard-stop → calibrate → pass (clean certificate, all attempts kept) or fail → retry up to 3 → supervisor disposition (back to hardware, or scrap).
  • QC pass is the single trigger that finalizes the record and authorizes shipping, subject to separation-of-duty (the signer cannot be the person who did the work, unless a manager logs a waiver).
